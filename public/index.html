What is a currency strength meter?

A currency strength meter shows how each currency is performing against a basket of the others, instead of in a single pair. That isolates the individual currency — so rather than only asking "is EUR/USD up?", you can see whether a move is EUR strength, USD weakness, or both. FXStrength tracks the 8 major currencies — USD, EUR, JPY, GBP, AUD, CHF, CAD, NZD — plus gold, silver and Bitcoin, live, across three timeframes at once.

How to read the meter

Each row is a currency (or gold, silver, Bitcoin); each column is a timeframe — intraday, daily and weekly. Scores run 0 (weakest) to 10 (strongest), shaded red → green, with an arrow for the short-term direction (▲ rising, ▼ falling, — flat). A row that's green across all three columns is aligned strong — the highest-conviction read.

How it's calculated

For each currency we take its percentage move against each of the other seven majors, across all 28 pairs, measured from the start of the window, and average them. A positive score means it has gained ground on the pack; a negative score means it has lost ground. Gold, silver and Bitcoin are measured the same way against the fiat basket, but their 0–10 score is scaled to each asset's own volatility — so a gold or Bitcoin reading is "strong for that asset," not directly comparable to the currencies.

How to use it

Look for currencies that are strong on the higher timeframes (weekly, daily) and pair them against currencies that are weak. When the intraday read agrees with the higher-timeframe trend, that alignment is where the highest-conviction moves tend to form. FXStrength is an information tool — what you do with it is your decision.

How often it updates

The data refreshes through the trading day (roughly every 15 minutes). FX markets are closed at weekends; Bitcoin trades 24/7, so there's always something live.

Frequently asked questions

What is currency strength?

What do the 0 to 10 scores and the arrows mean?

0 is the weakest and 10 the strongest; green means strong and red means weak. The arrow shows the short-term direction over recent bars: up means rising, down means falling, and a dash means flat.

How are gold, silver and Bitcoin scored?

They are scored against their own normal volatility, where 0 to 10 means weak to strong for that asset. A metal or crypto routinely moves several percent when the biggest fiat move is under one percent, so their score means "strong for that asset" and is not directly comparable to the currencies.
